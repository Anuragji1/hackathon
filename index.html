About

Considering the increasing burden of mental disorders, it's crucial to identify individuals at risk early. This web application aims to provide a platform for self-identifying mental health status and accessing guidance for support. Explore the following features:

  • Self-Assessment: Take an interactive assessment to evaluate your mental health status based on evidence-based criteria.
  • Personalized Recommendations: Receive personalized recommendations based on your assessment results to improve your mental well-being.
  • Resource Library: Access a comprehensive library of resources, including articles, videos, and self-help tools, curated by mental health professionals.
  • Community Support: Connect with a supportive community of individuals facing similar challenges, share experiences, and offer mutual support.

Resources

Access resources and support for mental health:


  • National Institute of Mental Health

    The National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) is the leading federal agency for research on mental disorders. They provide valuable information on various mental health conditions, treatment options, and research initiatives.


  • World Health Organization - Mental Health

    The World Health Organization (WHO) focuses on promoting mental health, preventing mental disorders, and improving treatment access globally. Their website offers resources, reports, and guidelines for mental health professionals and the general public.


  • American Psychiatric Association

    The American Psychiatric Association (APA) is a professional organization representing psychiatrists in the United States. They provide resources for both mental health professionals and individuals seeking information about mental health conditions and treatments.

  • MentalHealth.gov provides resources and information on mental health conditions, treatment options, and how to find help.
